The Proposal

His Side

I had bought the engagement ring on January 17th, 2017 thinking it would be the year that I proposed to Dana, but I didn’t know how to make it more special in my own way. When I heard my grandma and uncle were selling their house in Vancouver, I knew I had to take her there to visit the place I had so many fond and eventful memories of as a kid. I began researching for ideal engagement locations, and I decided on one of the most beautiful botanical gardens in the world, The Butchart Gardens.

Throughout the first half of the trip, I had to hide the ring in my backpack, and it was especially difficult to keep the ring hidden in my sweater on the way to the gardens. I proposed to her at the iconic Sunken Garden on August 16th, 2017. As nervous as I was, it was honestly the easiest decision I had to make in my entire life.

Her Side

Jay led me to believe the proposal would happen towards the end of the year (I kept asking him when it would happen since I was getting impatient), so I was actually completely surprised when he proposed. I had even been joking the day before about how I wouldn’t have minded if he proposed in Vancouver, and he acted as if that would have been really inconvenient since he’d have to bring the ring onto the plane, and I agreed, oblivious to his scheming. As we arrived at The Butchart Gardens, I had only questioned why he wanted to keep his sweater on since it wasn’t too chilly, and why he chose to stand on a specific side next to me when taking pictures. But distracted by the beauty of the gardens and trying not to trip everywhere I walked, I didn’t question him any further.

We walked up to a quieter spot, where we stood overlooking the Sunken Garden. It sounds a little cliche, but once he took my hands and began proposing, it felt like my surroundings were a blur, and all I could see and hear was him. Whenever I would daydream about him proposing, I would imagine myself teasing him by not answering immediately, or by responding with “I don’t know”, but when he got down on one knee and asked, I completely forgot and quickly said “yes”. Even though we had already moved into our new house and talked about marriage constantly (after dating for a year, I...well, I created an email account for the future Dana Chiao), there was a special kind of happiness I felt when he would introduce me as his fiance.
